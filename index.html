Research: My research focused on using quantitative/statistical genetic approaches to understand the evolution of sex differences. Males and females share many of the same genes which should make sex differences slow to evolve, but sex differences are rife in nature. I am interested in generation and resolution of the genetic conflict generated by selection towards divergent sex-specific optima. Statistical approaches allow us to estimate the genetic variance within traits, covariance among traits and between the sexes, populate genetic variance-covariance matrices, and assess the resulting evolutionary potential in a multivariate context using matrix comparison tools. More can be found in my PhD thesis and papers.
