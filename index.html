Profile: I am a programmer working on late-phase studies in respiratory and immunology diseases, father of two (soon three) and keen cyclist and runner. I became interested in evolutionary biology and research during my studies (BSc in Conservation Biology and Ecology, University of Exeter, 2010) and decided to pursue an academic career.

Research: I completed a masters (MSc Evolutionary and Behvioural Ecology, University of Exeter, 2011) and PhD (Biology with specialisation in Evolutionary Genetics, Uppsala University 2015), followed by postdoctoral postitions at the University of Turku, Uppsala University and the University of Queensland. My research focused on using quantitative/statistical genetic approaches to understand the evolution of sex differences. Males and females share many of the same genes which should make sex differences slow to evolve, but sex differences are rife in nature. Statistical approaches allow us to estimate the genetic vairance within traits, covariance among traits and between the sexes, populate genetic variance-covariance matrices, and assess the resulting evolutionary potential in a multivariate context using matrix comparison tools. More can be found in my PhD thesis and papers.
